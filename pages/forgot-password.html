A Symbol of India’s Rich History & Culture

About the Red Fort

The Red Fort, located in Delhi, is one of the most iconic monuments in India. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648, it served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. Its massive red sandstone walls stand as a reminder of India’s glorious past and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Highlights

  • Built in 1648 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Hosts India’s Independence Day celebrations every year
  • Massive red sandstone walls spanning 2.5 km
  • Intricate Mughal architecture and gardens
